Fried Chicken Recipe

Ingredients

  • 4-6 chicken pieces (e.g. drumsticks, thighs, breasts)
  • 1 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon of garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon of onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup of buttermilk
  • Cooking oil for frying

    • Instructions

  • In a large bowl, mix together the flour, garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  • Pour the buttermilk into a separate bowl.
  • Dip each chicken piece in the flour mixture, making sure to coat it well.
  • Shake off any excess flour and dip the chicken piece into the buttermilk.
  • Dip the chicken piece back into the flour mixture, making sure to coat it well again.
  • Place the chicken pieces on a wire rack to let them rest for about 10-15 minutes. This will help the coating adhere better to the chicken.
  • Heat a large skillet or a deep fryer with cooking oil over medium-high heat.
  • Once the oil is hot, carefully place the chicken pieces in the skillet or fryer, making sure not to overcrowd the pan.
  • Fry the chicken for about 12-15 minutes or until the coating is crispy and golden brown, and the chicken is cooked
    through. You can check if the chicken is cooked by inserting a thermometer into the thickest part of the chicken, which should read at least 165°F (74°C).
  • Use a slotted spoon or tongs to remove the chicken from the skillet or fryer and place it on a wire rack or paper towels to drain any excess oil.
  • Serve hot with your favorite sides, such as mashed potatoes, coleslaw, or biscuits.
